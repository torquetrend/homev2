Tesla Model S Review

By Sarah Thompson | Published on October 31, 2024

The Tesla Model S has redefined the automotive industry since its introduction, setting new standards for electric vehicles in terms of performance, technology, and design. The latest Model S continues this trend, offering a combination of speed, range, and innovation that keeps it at the forefront of the EV market.

Design and Exterior

The Model S features a sleek and minimalist design that balances elegance with aerodynamic efficiency. The updated exterior includes a refreshed front fascia, new wheel designs, and subtle tweaks that enhance its modern appeal.

Key Exterior Features

  • Full LED lighting with adaptive headlights
  • Panoramic glass roof
  • Flush door handles

Interior and Comfort

The interior of the Model S is a blend of luxury and futuristic minimalism. The spacious cabin provides ample room for passengers, and the minimalist dashboard centers around a large touchscreen interface. High-quality materials and attention to detail contribute to a premium feel.

Technology Highlights

  • 17-inch central touchscreen with updated UI
  • 12.3-inch digital instrument cluster
  • Yoke steering wheel (optional)
  • Full Self-Driving capability (additional cost)

Performance and Range

The Model S offers several performance variants, with the top-of-the-line Plaid model delivering jaw-dropping acceleration. The dual or tri-motor setups provide instant torque and all-wheel-drive capability.

Performance Specifications

  • Long Range: 0-60 mph in 3.1 seconds, top speed of 155 mph
  • Plaid: 0-60 mph in under 2 seconds, top speed of 200 mph

The estimated range varies by model, with the Long Range offering up to 405 miles on a single charge, making it one of the longest-range electric vehicles available.

“The Model S Plaid is not just fast for an electric car; it's one of the fastest production cars ever made.” — Automotive Journalist

Charging and Infrastructure

Tesla's Supercharger network provides fast and convenient charging options for Model S owners. The vehicle is compatible with V3 Superchargers, allowing for rapid charging speeds.

Autopilot and Safety

The Model S comes equipped with Tesla's Autopilot system, offering advanced driver-assistance features. Safety is a strong focus, with a 5-star safety rating and robust construction.

Safety Features

  • Automatic Emergency Braking
  • Lane Departure Warning and Prevention
  • Adaptive Cruise Control

Verdict

The Tesla Model S remains a benchmark in the electric vehicle market. Its combination of performance, range, and technology is unmatched, offering a compelling option for those seeking a premium electric sedan.

Pros

  • Exceptional acceleration and performance
  • Long electric range
  • Advanced technology and over-the-air updates
  • Extensive charging network

Cons

  • High purchase price
  • Minimalist interior may not appeal to everyone
  • Availability of certain features may vary by region
